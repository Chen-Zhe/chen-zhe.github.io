Chen Zhe's Photo Portfolio

Preface

Photography is not just about art. Indeed, composition matters a lot in making a photo appear pleasing, so does location, color and postprocessing. To me, photography is more than that, because every time I press down the shutter button and the curtains click, it captures a moment that I can never relive again. Be it a moment of joy, surprise, tranquility or sorrow, photo is a gate to the past and allows me to re-immerse in the emotion captured by the photo. It's from the past that I can gain experience and strength to fight in the current.

Night time at Downtown Manhattan

Taken on the 86th floor observatory of Empire State Building in a 30-second exposure, which turned out to be a bit too long as the lights on the One World Trade Center are slightly blown out. I usually try to make use of objects at hand as tripod while travelling, and this time, my tripod is the steel fence of the observatory. Most of the openings on the fence is not big enough to fit in a DSLR camera lens, but the opening at the base is just enough for my Canon 24-70mm f/4 to slide through and firmly attached for this moment.

4000m above Guangdong Province

Taken aboard a China Southern Airlines flight from Singapore to Guangzhou, China. For a long time, China Southern is the choice for me to go back home during term breaks, with relatively good timing and fast layover. Not to mention that their IATA code, CZ, is the same as my initial :)
This photo will serve as a reminder to me, that although I have real-life limitations, my mind can still fly high above the ground and take a walk in the clouds.

Thunder

Thunder is one of the hardest event to capture and I was lucky to get this shot. This is a single 15-second exposure and the only postprocessing done was to dramatically recover the highlight (luckily I always shoot raw, albeit with drawbacks, too) and adjust the white balance.

Fireworks, Singapore National Day Parade rehearsal

Fireworks are traditionally taken using Black Card techniques to stack more fireworks onto a single frame. However, with digital photography and Photoshop, this can be done in postprocessing, with more granularity for control. This photo is a controlled stack of 4 images. If you ever wonder why is the sky dark blue while the fireworks are going on, it’s because I chose to have this sky in the stack so that the sky doesn’t look too empty.
