Chen Zhe's Photo Portfolio

To me, photography is more than just art. Because every time the shutter curtains click, my camera captures a moment that I can never relive again. Be it a moment of joy, surprise, tranquility or sorrow, photo is a window to the past, and it's from the past that I can gain experience and strength to fight in the present.

Night time at Downtown Manhattan Taken on the 86th floor observatory of Empire State Building in a 30-second exposure, which turned out to be a bit too long as the lights on the One World Trade Center are slightly blown out. I usually try to make use of objects at hand as tripod while travelling, and this time, my tripod is the steel fence of the observatory. Most of the openings on the fence is not big enough to fit in a DSLR camera lens, but the opening at the base is just enough for my Canon 24-70mm f/4 to slide through and firmly attached for this moment.
4000m above Guangdong Province Taken aboard a China Southern Airlines flight from Singapore to Guangzhou, China. For a long time, China Southern is the choice for me to go back home during term breaks, with relatively good timing and fast layover. Not to mention that their IATA code, CZ, is the same as my initial :)
This photo will serve as a reminder to me, that although I have real-life limitations, my mind can still fly high above the ground and take a walk in the clouds.
Thunder Thunder is one of the hardest event to capture and I was lucky to get this shot. This is a single 15-second exposure and the only postprocessing done was to dramatically recover the highlight (luckily I always shoot raw, albeit with drawbacks, too) and adjust the white balance.
The Hive, Nanyang Technological University a stitch panorama (with some artifacts) of the interior of an oddly-shaped building in NTU
Fireworks, Singapore National Day Parade rehearsal Fireworks are traditionally taken using Black Card techniques to stack more fireworks onto a single frame. However, with digital photography and Photoshop, this can be done in postprocessing, with more granularity for control. This photo is a controlled stack of 4 image. If you ever wonder why is the sky dark blue while the fireworks are going on, it’s because I chose to have this sky in the stack so that the sky doesn’t look too empty.
Lotus Peak, Huangshan, Anhui A panorama at sunrise. The color is magical!
Flowers of an ordinary type of vegetable Things look more intriguing in Macro, don’t you think?
Epic sunset in a small town Taken at my grandma’s place, a small county in Jiangxi Province. It’s a stitched panorama and have the horizon adjusted to appear even more epic
Black and White, Mount Longhu, Jiangxi The color is a bit dull on that day, so I thought: hey, why not make it black and white, so that the color doesn’t matter anymore?
