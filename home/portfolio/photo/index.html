Chen Zhe's Photo Portfolio

To me, photography is more than just art. Because every time the shutter curtains click, my camera captures a moment that I can never relive again. Be it a moment of joy, surprise, tranquility or sorrow, photo is a window to the past, and it’s from the past that I can gain experience and strength to fight in the present.

You can browse through my curated photos using the navigation buttons, touch swipes, scroll wheel, or ←→ arrow keys. Touch/click the photo title to see some interesting facts about the photo.

Night time at Downtown Manhattan
  • Canon EOS 6D
  • EF24-70mm f/4L IS USM @ 59mm
  • S.S. 30s
  • A. f/11
  • ISO 100
  • Take at 2015/6/14 20:49
Taken on the 86th floor observatory of Empire State Building in a 30-second exposure, which turned out to be a bit too long as the lights on the One World Trade Center are slightly blown out. I usually try to make use of objects at hand as tripod while travelling, and this time, my tripod is the steel fence of the observatory. Most of the openings on the fence are not big enough to fit in a DSLR camera lens, but the opening at the base is just enough for my Canon 24-70mm f/4 to poke right through and firmly locked for this moment.

Fireworks, Singapore National Day Parade rehearsal
  • FUJIFILM X-T20
  • XF10-24mmF4 R OIS @ 10mm
  • S.S. 2.5s
  • A. f/13
  • ISO 200
  • Take at 2018/7/7 20:19
Fireworks are traditionally taken using Black Card techniques to stack more fireworks onto a single frame. However, with digital photography and Photoshop, this can be done in postprocessing, with more granularity for control. This photo is a controlled stack of 4 image. If you ever wonder why is the sky dark blue while the fireworks are going on, it’s because I chose to have this sky in the stack so that the sky doesn’t look too empty.
